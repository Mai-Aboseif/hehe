    Hey conventionalist friends,
    Having a learning system to learn about environmental science, or mastering any subject, is crucial as it allows you to anticipate the whole trajectory and link it to a larger purpose rather than hitting random goals. Especially in environmental science, effective learning requires a flexible mindset to easily zoom in on the nitty-gritty details and zoom out to see the broader picture. My system, particularly, allows you to stay motivated, consistent, and organized through:

    1. Fantasy realization

      Fantasy realization is a psychological framework developed by researcher Gabriele Oettingen that helps people turn dreams into achievable outcomes. At its core, it’s about managing the tension between dreaming big and reality. Fantasy realization involves a deliberate mental process called Mental Contrasting that’s functioning based on visualizing success and acknowledging obstacles, which is where the system takes place. My system provides you with a roadmap that includes both a clear destination and strategies for overcoming challenges.

    2. Self-Determination Theory

      We've all experienced those moments when we set goals and create detailed plans but eventually find ourselves procrastinating. Why does this happen? More often than not, this procrastination stems from a lack of courage or, in other words, you’re not motivated for the task ahead.
      There are two types of motivation: intrinsic and extrinsic. Self-determination theory explains how we can leverage intrinsic motivation to create a sustainable motivation system. It focuses on three key psychological factors that drive effective
      1. Autonomy: feeling in control of your behaviors and goals. This sense of being able to take direct action that will result in real change plays a major part in helping people feel self-determined.
      2. Competence: – Having the skills or developing the ability to succeed in the task. When people feel that they have the skills needed for success, they are more likely to take actions that will help them achieve their goals.
      3. Relatedness: Feeling a sense of connection to others or the broader impact of your work. Dr. Laurie Santos, a Yale professor, explained how appreciating our Common Humanity (common human struggles) can help us overcome the lack of courage and stay consistent with our goals.
      Thus, Mai Bicycle offers you a workplace that provides you with self-determination theory components.

    3. How This Translates to Environmental Science

      Applying this theory to environmental science (or any field) means creating a learning system where each milestone feels purposeful and connected to the bigger vision. Autonomy ensures you’re steering your learning journey; competence keeps you improving, and relatedness reminds you why it all matters—whether it’s the ecosystems you’re protecting or the communities you’re empowering.
      This shift doesn’t just help you stay consistent; it makes the process fulfilling. Because in the end, motivation isn’t just about pushing through; it’s about finding joy and meaning in the journey itself.
