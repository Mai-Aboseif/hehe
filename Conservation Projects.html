Research Abstract: Inclusive E-Bike Solutions


In the context of climate change, unsustainable modes of transportation contribute to 1/4 of CO2 emissions, emphasizing our need for cycling. However, cycling among adults and disabled individuals is significantly limited due to physical, cultural, and societal barriers. This research examines these disparities, introducing an accessible solution.

Research Abstract: Sustainable Energy Harvesting from Human Movement


In the context of rising energy consumption and environmental concerns, government facilities are among the largest energy consumers. This research introduces an innovative energy-harvesting device that converts kinetic energy from human congestion into electrical power. The proposed solution integrates scalable and durable energy conversion technology designed for high-foot-traffic areas.
This study focuses on addressing energy dependency and promoting sustainability by transforming human activity into a renewable energy source. The implementation of this technology aims to redefine energy use in public spaces, making it more efficient and environmentally friendly.
